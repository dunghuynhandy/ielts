# IELTS Speaking — Tips & Tricks for Band 7

> Practical guide from **IELTS Fighter (Fighter 5)** + **IELTS Simon** topic ideas.
> Target: **Band 7 overall** — use with [examples](examples/README.md) and [vocabulary](vocabulary/22_06_2026.md).

---

## What Band 7 actually means

Your overall score is the average of four criteria. You need **7.0 in each** (or balance one weaker area with a stronger one).

| Criterion | Band 6 (where you might be stuck) | Band 7 (what you need to show) |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | Can keep going but with repetition or hesitation | Speak at length **without noticeable effort**; ideas connect clearly |
| **Lexical Resource** | Enough vocab for familiar topics | **Flexible** vocab across topics; some **less common words** + natural collocations |
| **Grammar** | Mix of short sentences; errors sometimes block meaning | **Range of complex structures**; mostly error-free, small mistakes OK |
| **Pronunciation** | Generally clear | Clear throughout; **easy to understand** with natural stress |

**Band 7 rule:** You don't need perfect English. You need **confident, extended, organised** answers with **some** advanced vocabulary and grammar.

---

## Band 6 → Band 7: the 3 biggest gaps

| Gap | Band 6 habit | Band 7 fix |
|-----|--------------|------------|
| **Length** | Short answers (1 sentence) | Part 1: 2–4 sentences · Part 2: full 2 min · Part 3: 4–6 sentences |
| **Structure** | Ideas jump around | Use **EPACT** (Part 1), **ISO** (Part 2), **ARERC** (Part 3) |
| **Depth** | Only talk about yourself in Part 3 | Talk about **people in general**, society, trends |

---

## Test structure

| Part | Time | Band 7 goal |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Part 1** | 4–5 min | Every answer extended — never one word |
| **Part 2** | 3–4 min | All bullet points + speak ~2 minutes |
| **Part 3** | 4–5 min | Opinion + reason + example every time |

## Part 1 — reach Band 7

### Stop doing this (Band 5–6)
*"Do you like music?" → "Yes, I do."*

### Start doing this (Band 7)
*"Yes, I'm quite into music actually. I mostly listen to pop and R&B when I'm commuting, because it helps me relax after a long day at work."*

→ **3 sentences**: direct answer + detail + reason.

### EPACT framework (Lesson 2)

Pick **2–3 steps** per answer — you don't need all five.

| Step | What to add | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **E**xample | Something from your life | *For example, I use Instagram to keep in touch with friends abroad.* |
| **P**opularity | How common it is | *It's pretty common these days — most people I know use it.* |
| **A**dvantage | A positive | *The main advantage is staying connected easily.* |
| **C**ost | A downside | *On the other hand, it can waste a lot of time.* |
| **T**ime | Past or future | *I used to scroll a lot, but now I limit it to 30 minutes.* |

### Wh-question extend trick (Lesson 1)

| Question | Band 7 pattern |
|----------|----------------|
| What / Which | Answer + describe + short opinion |
| Who | Who they are + one trait + why they matter to you |
| When / Where | Time/place + what happened + how you felt |
| Why | Reason + *for example* + result |
| How often | Frequency + when in your routine |

### Band 7 Part 1 phrases (simple, natural)

- *To be honest…*
- *It really depends, but generally…*
- *I'd say…*
- *The main reason is…*
- *For instance…*

### Useful vocab (Lessons 1–2)

**Daily life:** contentment, mundane, tedious, procrastinator, get distracted

**Technology:** ubiquitous, binge watching, `come in handy`, `keep abreast of the news`

**Transport:** congestion, carbon footprint, `mass transit`, `traffic congestion`

## Part 2 — reach Band 7

### Stop doing this (Band 5–6)
Stopping at 45–60 seconds, or missing cue card bullets.

### Start doing this (Band 7)
Speak for **~2 minutes**, cover **every bullet**, end with feelings or a recommendation.

### 1-minute prep — write keywords only

```
What/Who:  _______
When:      _______
Where:     _______
Detail:    _______
Feeling:   _______
```

### Band 7 structure: ISO

```
INTRO   (15 sec)  → "I'd like to talk about…"
STORY   (75 sec)  → Cover all bullet points with details
OPINION (30 sec)  → How you feel + would you recommend it?
```

### Cue card types (Lessons 3–7)

| Type | What to include for Band 7 |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Person** | Appearance + 2 personality traits + one memory |
| **Place** | Atmosphere + what you do there + sensory detail |
| **Thing** | How you got it + why it matters to you |
| **Book/film** | Brief plot + why it affected you (don't retell everything) |
| **Past event** | First → then → finally (past tense throughout) |
| **Hobby** | How often + benefits + `let off steam` |
| **Future plan** | Where/when + who with + why you're excited |

### If you run out of things to say

1. Compare: *It's similar to… / Unlike…*
2. Contrast past & now: *I used to…, but now…*
3. Mention others: *Most of my friends…*
4. Look ahead: *I hope in the future…*

### Band 7 collocations

**Places:** `once in a lifetime experience`, `to die for`, `packed like sardines`

**Things:** `worth every penny`, `from cover to cover`, `catch one's eye`

**Events:** `have a blast`, `get carried away`, `a sense of camaraderie`

**Activities:** `let off steam`, `do wonders for`, `get itchy feet`

### Part 2 timing (aim for this)

| Time | Content |
|------|---------|
| 0:00–0:15 | Introduction |
| 0:15–1:30 | Story — all bullets |
| 1:30–2:00 | Opinion + ending |

## Part 3 — reach Band 7

### Stop doing this (Band 5–6)
Short personal answers: *"I think it's good because I like it."*

### Start doing this (Band 7)
*"I think governments should invest more in public transport, because it reduces traffic congestion. For example, in my city, the new metro line has made commuting much faster for thousands of people."*

→ **Opinion + reason + example** — that's Band 7 Part 3.

### ARERC framework (Lesson 8)

| Step | What to do |
|------|------------|
| **A**nswer | Clear yes/no or position |
| **R**eason | Why — give 1–2 reasons |
| **E**xample | Real example (your country, people you know, news) |
| **C**onsequence | What happens as a result |

### 7 question types — Band 7 approach

| Type | How to answer |
|------|---------------|
| **Opinion** | Yes/no → reason → example |
| **Evaluation** | How important (very/quite/a little) → explain why |
| **Future** | What will change → why → your prediction |
| **Cause & effect** | Main reason + second reason |
| **Hypothetical** | If X happened → then Y |
| **Compare** | Difference A vs B → example |
| **Other people** | *Many people think… However…* |

### Band 7 Part 3 phrases

**Buying time:**
- *That's an interesting question…*
- *I haven't thought about that before, but…*
- *As far as I'm concerned…*

**Balanced view (good for Band 7):**
- *It really depends…*
- *On the one hand… on the other hand…*
- *There are pros and cons…*

**Talk about society:**
- *In my country…*
- *Many people believe…*
- *Governments should…*

### Use Simon ideas for Part 3

Read **Positives / Negatives / Opinions** in [examples/](examples/README.md) for your topic. Pick 1–2 ideas and build an ARERC answer around them.

## Band 7 checklist (all parts)

### Fluency
- [ ] No long awkward silences — use fillers (*well, actually, you know*)
- [ ] Connect ideas: *because, however, for example, as a result*
- [ ] Self-correct once if needed, then keep going

### Vocabulary
- [ ] Use **collocations** from [vocabulary](vocabulary/22_06_2026.md) — not isolated words
- [ ] Paraphrase the question at the start
- [ ] 1–2 less common words per answer is enough (e.g. *mundane*, *beneficial*, *congestion*)

### Grammar
- [ ] Mix short + longer sentences
- [ ] Add **one** complex structure per answer: relative clause (*the person who…*) or conditional (*if people…, they will…*)
- [ ] Small mistakes are OK at Band 7 — don't panic

### Pronunciation
- [ ] Speak clearly — examiner must understand you easily
- [ ] Stress important words, don't speak in a flat voice
- [ ] Don't worry about a perfect accent

## Band 6 mistakes that block Band 7

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| One-word Part 1 answers | Always 2–4 sentences |
| Part 2 under 1 minute | Use "keep talking" tricks |
| Part 3 too personal | Say *people*, *society*, *in my country* |
| Memorised scripts | Learn frameworks, not full answers |
| Wrong tense in past story | Past event = past tense only |
| Missing cue card bullets | Check each bullet while speaking |

## Exam day — Band 7 mindset

**Before**
- Review EPACT + ARERC once out loud
- Know your Part 1 topics: job, hometown, hobbies

**During**
- Part 1: extend every answer
- Part 2: use full 1 min prep; aim for 2 min
- Part 3: opinion → reason → example
- Don't understand? *Could you repeat the question, please?*
- Made a mistake? Fix it once, move on

## Lesson reference (IELTS Fighter)

### Lesson 1 — Part 1 Wh-questions
Extend every answer. Vocab: gifted, innate, contentment, tedious, `stick in one's mind`, `make one's day`

### Lesson 2 — Part 1 EPACT
Tech & transport topics. Vocab: ubiquitous, congestion, `come in handy`, `carbon footprint`

### Lesson 3 — Describe a person
Intro → appearance → personality → influence. Phrases: *In terms of appearance… / Personality-wise…*

### Lesson 4 — Describe a place
Sensory details + atmosphere. Collocations: `a panoramic view of`, `delectable cuisines`

### Lesson 5 — Describe a thing
Physical: how you got it → features → emotional value. Book/film: plot summary → life lesson

### Lesson 6 — Past event
Sequencing: *First… Then… Eventually…* Collocations: `have a blast`, `grab the chance`

### Lesson 7 — Activities
Present: how often + benefits. Future: dream + excitement. `let off steam`, `get itchy feet`

### Lesson 8 — Part 3 ARERC
7 question types + buying time phrases (see Part 3 section above)

### Lesson 9 — Part 3 practice
Balance phrases: *On the one hand… / While some people…*

### Lesson 10 — Review Part 2 + 3
Do full mocks: cue card → Part 3 follow-ups as one connected practice

## Quick reference — Band 7

```
PART 1:  Answer + detail + reason/example  (2–4 sentences)
         EPACT when useful

PART 2:  Intro → Story (all bullets) → Opinion  (~2 min)
         Prep: keywords only

PART 3:  Answer → Reason → Example → Result
         Think society, not just "I think…"
```

| Part | Framework |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | **EPACT** or **extend** (answer + why + example) |
| 2 | **ISO** (Intro → Story → Opinion) |
| 3 | **ARERC** (Answer → Reason → Example → Consequence) |
