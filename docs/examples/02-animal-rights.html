Animal Rights

Sources: NgocBach Q1 2024, NgocBach Q3

Key Ideas (IELTS Simon)

Positives

  • Zoos play an important role in wildlife conservation.
  • They can help to protect endangered species.
  • Zoos allow scientists to study animals and their behaviour.
  • Zoos are educational, interesting and fun.
  • Children, in particular, enjoy learning about animals.
  • Zoos provide job opportunities.

Negatives

  • Zoo animals are kept in artificial environments.
  • They are kept in cages or have limited space.
  • Zoo animals rely on humans.
  • They lose the freedom to hunt for food.
  • The best way to save endangered species is by protecting natural habitats.
  • Some people believe that zoos are unethical.
  • Zoos exhibit animals with the aim of making money.
  • We have no right to use animals for entertainment and profit.

Cue card 1

Describe a time you saw an animal in an unusual place or situation

You should say: - what animal it was - where you saw it - what it was doing - and explain how you felt about it

Part 3 follow-up questions: 1. Is it right to use animals for scientific testing? 2. What are the arguments for and against keeping animals in zoos? 3. Why do some people choose to become vegetarian? 4. Should governments do more to protect endangered species? 5. How can we reduce cruelty to animals?

Cue card 2

Describe a pet or animal you like

You should say: - what animal it is - where you usually see it - what it looks like - and explain why you like it

Part 3 follow-up questions: 1. What are the benefits of keeping pets? 2. Should children be taught to care for animals? 3. Why do some people prefer exotic pets?

  2. Should hunting be banned?
  3. How can zoos improve the lives of animals?
