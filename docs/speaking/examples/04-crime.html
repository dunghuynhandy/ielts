Crime

Sources: NgocBach Q3

Key Ideas (IELTS Simon)

Negatives

  • Criminals are put together.
  • They make friends with other offenders.
  • Many prisoners re-offend when they are released.
  • A criminal record makes finding a job more difficult.
  • Another aim of prisons is rehabilitation.
  • Prisoners receive education or vocational training.
  • Prisoners should learn personal skills and specific job skills.
  • Punishment could make prisoners’ behaviour worse.
  • Rehabilitation aims to make them better citizens.
  • Rehabilitated prisoners are less likely to re-offend.
  • Capital Punishment
  • Supporters say that capital punishment deters crime.
  • Fear of the death penalty stops people from committing offences.
  • The death penalty shows that crime is not tolerated.
  • It is a form of revenge.
  • The cost of imprisonment is avoided.
  • The offender cannot pose a threat to others.
  • Against Capital Punishment
  • Innocent people could be wrongly convicted and executed.
  • Crime rates are not necessarily reduced.
  • Many criminals do not think they will be caught.
  • Capital punishment is not a good deterrent.
  • Executing prisoners creates a violent culture and encourages revenge.
  • We have no right to take another human life.
  • Community Service
  • Community service is a way to reform offenders.
  • It could be a solution to prison overcrowding.
  • It avoids the cost of imprisonment.
  • It makes offenders useful in their local communities.
  • They are required to clean streets or talk to school groups.
  • Offenders repay their community.
  • They avoid the negative influence that prison can have.
  • Against Community Service
  • Community service is not a sufficient punishment.
  • Criminals should be locked up, away from their victims.
  • Crime in the Media
  • Crime is one of the main subjects of most news programmes.
  • The mass media focus on violent and sensational crimes.
  • This leads to fear of crime among the public.
  • Sensational stories attract more viewers or readers.
  • The media report crime stories in order to increase their audience.

Sample Answers Band 6.5–7.0

Part 1 — Crime

1. Are you interested in public safety?

Yes, to some extent. I would not say I am an expert, but public safety affects daily life, so I find it worth thinking about. For example, when I read news or talk with friends, this topic often comes up naturally.

2. Is public safety important in your country?

Yes, definitely. In my country, public safety matters because it influences families, young people, and the wider community. It may not be something people discuss every day, but it has a real impact on quality of life.

3. Has public safety changed in recent years?

Yes, quite a lot. Technology, education, and modern lifestyles have changed the way people think about public safety. Older people may have more traditional views, while younger people tend to be more open and practical.

4. Do young people care about public safety?

I think many young people do care, especially when it affects their future directly. However, they may express interest through social media or online discussions rather than traditional conversations.

Part 2 — Cue card sample

An important rule at school or work

I would like to talk about an important rule at school or work, which is closely connected to public safety.

I first became aware of it a few years ago, and since then it has stayed in my mind because it reflects both the positive and negative sides of modern life.

The positive side is that it shows the value of clear laws and crime prevention. This is important because people often need practical examples, not just abstract ideas, to understand a topic clearly.

At the same time, it also reminds me of the problem of poverty, unemployment, and weak enforcement. That makes the topic more complex, because there is rarely a perfect solution.

Overall, I find it meaningful because it helps me think more deeply about society and my own choices. If I had to summarise my feeling, I would say it is both interesting and relevant to everyday life.

Part 3 — Discussion

1. Why is public safety important?

It is important because it affects individuals and society at the same time. For example, clear laws and crime prevention can improve peoples quality of life, while poverty, unemployment, and weak enforcement can create long-term problems. That is why this topic deserves more attention from both citizens and governments.

2. What are the advantages related to public safety?

The main advantages are connected to clear laws and crime prevention. If this area is managed well, people can benefit through better opportunities, more convenience, and a higher standard of living. It can also help communities become more organised and forward-thinking.

3. What are the disadvantages or risks?

The biggest risk is poverty, unemployment, and weak enforcement. If people ignore this issue, it can lead to social pressure, inequality, or environmental damage depending on the situation. In my view, the negative side should not be exaggerated, but it should be taken seriously.

4. What should governments do about public safety?

Governments should create clear policies, invest in public education, and make sure rules are enforced fairly. For example, they can fund useful programmes and raise public awareness. However, government action alone is not enough; individuals also need to change their habits.

5. Do you think public safety will change in the future?

Yes, I think it will continue to change, mainly because of technology, globalisation, and younger generations. People are becoming more aware of both benefits and risks. In the future, I expect more practical solutions rather than simple arguments for or against it.

6. Do older and younger people think differently about public safety?

Yes, in many cases. Older people may value tradition, stability, and personal experience, while younger people are often influenced by online information and global trends. Neither side is completely right or wrong; the best approach is to combine experience with fresh ideas.
