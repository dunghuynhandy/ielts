Sample Answers Band 6.5–7.0

Part 1 — Environment

1. Are you interested in protecting nature?

Yes, to some extent. I would not say I am an expert, but protecting nature affects daily life, so I find it worth thinking about. For example, when I read news or talk with friends, this topic often comes up naturally.

2. Is protecting nature important in your country?

Yes, definitely. In my country, protecting nature matters because it influences families, young people, and the wider community. It may not be something people discuss every day, but it has a real impact on quality of life.

3. Has protecting nature changed in recent years?

Yes, quite a lot. Technology, education, and modern lifestyles have changed the way people think about protecting nature. Older people may have more traditional views, while younger people tend to be more open and practical.

4. Do young people care about protecting nature?

I think many young people do care, especially when it affects their future directly. However, they may express interest through social media or online discussions rather than traditional conversations.

Part 2 — Cue card sample

An important plant in my country

I would like to talk about an important plant in my country, which is closely connected to protecting nature.

I first became aware of it a few years ago, and since then it has stayed in my mind because it reflects both the positive and negative sides of modern life.

The positive side is that it shows the value of clean air, recycling, and conservation. This is important because people often need practical examples, not just abstract ideas, to understand a topic clearly.

At the same time, it also reminds me of the problem of pollution and climate change. That makes the topic more complex, because there is rarely a perfect solution.

Overall, I find it meaningful because it helps me think more deeply about society and my own choices. If I had to summarise my feeling, I would say it is both interesting and relevant to everyday life.

Part 3 — Discussion

1. Why is protecting nature important?

It is important because it affects individuals and society at the same time. For example, clean air, recycling, and conservation can improve peoples quality of life, while pollution and climate change can create long-term problems. That is why this topic deserves more attention from both citizens and governments.

2. What are the advantages related to protecting nature?

The main advantages are connected to clean air, recycling, and conservation. If this area is managed well, people can benefit through better opportunities, more convenience, and a higher standard of living. It can also help communities become more organised and forward-thinking.

3. What are the disadvantages or risks?

The biggest risk is pollution and climate change. If people ignore this issue, it can lead to social pressure, inequality, or environmental damage depending on the situation. In my view, the negative side should not be exaggerated, but it should be taken seriously.

4. What should governments do about protecting nature?

Governments should create clear policies, invest in public education, and make sure rules are enforced fairly. For example, they can fund useful programmes and raise public awareness. However, government action alone is not enough; individuals also need to change their habits.

5. Do you think protecting nature will change in the future?

Yes, I think it will continue to change, mainly because of technology, globalisation, and younger generations. People are becoming more aware of both benefits and risks. In the future, I expect more practical solutions rather than simple arguments for or against it.

6. Do older and younger people think differently about protecting nature?

Yes, in many cases. Older people may value tradition, stability, and personal experience, while younger people are often influenced by online information and global trends. Neither side is completely right or wrong; the best approach is to combine experience with fresh ideas.
