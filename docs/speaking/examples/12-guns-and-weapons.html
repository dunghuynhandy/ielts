Part 1 (5 questions)

  1. Are guns common in your country?
  2. Do you think weapons appear too often in films and games?
  3. Have you ever seen a weapon in real life?
  4. Do you feel safe from violence where you live?
  5. Do you think violent video games affect young people?

Part 2 — Cue Cards (2 cards)

Cue card 1

Describe a film or game that involves weapons or fighting

You should say: - what it is - what happens in it - who you watched or played it with - and explain how you felt about it

Part 3 follow-up questions: 1. Should ordinary citizens be allowed to own guns? 2. What role should the police play regarding weapons? 3. How can the international arms trade be controlled? 4. Are nuclear weapons necessary for national security? 5. Should violent content be restricted in media?

Cue card 2

Describe a news story about violence or conflict you remember

You should say: - what the story was about - where and when it happened - how you heard about it - and explain how it made you feel

Part 3 follow-up questions: 1. Why does violence appear so often in the news? 2. How can countries reduce armed conflict? 3. Should military spending be reduced?

Part 3 — Discussion (4 questions)

  1. What are the arguments for and against gun ownership?
  2. How can governments prevent illegal weapons trafficking?
  3. Should armed forces be used for peacekeeping abroad?
  4. Do films and games contribute to real-world violence?
