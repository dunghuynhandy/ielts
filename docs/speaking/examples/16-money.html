Sources: NgocBach Q1 2022, NgocBach Q1 2024, NgocBach Q3

Key Ideas (IELTS Simon)

Positives

  • Consumerism creates employment.
  • It helps to reduce poverty.
  • It encourages innovation and creativity in business.
  • We live in a global economy.
  • We have a better quality of life.

Negatives

  • Consumerist societies create more waste.
  • They use more natural resources.
  • They cause damage to the environment.
  • Consumerism creates a “throw-away” culture.
  • Advertisers tell us who we are and what we want.
  • Wealth does not lead to happiness.
  • Materialism causes greed and crime.
  • We should return to traditional values like sharing.

Sample Answers Band 6.5–7.0

Part 1 — Money

1. Are you interested in saving and spending?

Yes, to some extent. I would not say I am an expert, but saving and spending affects daily life, so I find it worth thinking about. For example, when I read news or talk with friends, this topic often comes up naturally.

2. Is saving and spending important in your country?

Yes, definitely. In my country, saving and spending matters because it influences families, young people, and the wider community. It may not be something people discuss every day, but it has a real impact on quality of life.

3. Has saving and spending changed in recent years?

Yes, quite a lot. Technology, education, and modern lifestyles have changed the way people think about saving and spending. Older people may have more traditional views, while younger people tend to be more open and practical.

4. Do young people care about saving and spending?

I think many young people do care, especially when it affects their future directly. However, they may express interest through social media or online discussions rather than traditional conversations.

Part 2 — Cue card sample

Something expensive i bought

I would like to talk about something expensive I bought, which is closely connected to saving and spending.

I first became aware of it a few years ago, and since then it has stayed in my mind because it reflects both the positive and negative sides of modern life.

The positive side is that it shows the value of financial security and useful purchases. This is important because people often need practical examples, not just abstract ideas, to understand a topic clearly.

At the same time, it also reminds me of the problem of consumerism and impulse buying. That makes the topic more complex, because there is rarely a perfect solution.

Overall, I find it meaningful because it helps me think more deeply about society and my own choices. If I had to summarise my feeling, I would say it is both interesting and relevant to everyday life.

Part 3 — Discussion

1. Why is saving and spending important?

It is important because it affects individuals and society at the same time. For example, financial security and useful purchases can improve peoples quality of life, while consumerism and impulse buying can create long-term problems. That is why this topic deserves more attention from both citizens and governments.

2. What are the advantages related to saving and spending?

The main advantages are connected to financial security and useful purchases. If this area is managed well, people can benefit through better opportunities, more convenience, and a higher standard of living. It can also help communities become more organised and forward-thinking.

3. What are the disadvantages or risks?

The biggest risk is consumerism and impulse buying. If people ignore this issue, it can lead to social pressure, inequality, or environmental damage depending on the situation. In my view, the negative side should not be exaggerated, but it should be taken seriously.

4. What should governments do about saving and spending?

Governments should create clear policies, invest in public education, and make sure rules are enforced fairly. For example, they can fund useful programmes and raise public awareness. However, government action alone is not enough; individuals also need to change their habits.

5. Do you think saving and spending will change in the future?

Yes, I think it will continue to change, mainly because of technology, globalisation, and younger generations. People are becoming more aware of both benefits and risks. In the future, I expect more practical solutions rather than simple arguments for or against it.

6. Do older and younger people think differently about saving and spending?

Yes, in many cases. Older people may value tradition, stability, and personal experience, while younger people are often influenced by online information and global trends. Neither side is completely right or wrong; the best approach is to combine experience with fresh ideas.
