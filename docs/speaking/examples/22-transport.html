Transport

Sources: NgocBach Q1 2022, NgocBach Q1 2024, NgocBach Q2 2022 (May–Sep), NgocBach Q3

Key Ideas (IELTS Simon)

Positives

  • We need to reduce our dependence on cars.
  • Parking a car can be extremely difficult in big cities.
  • Well-designed transport systems are comfortable and convenient.
  • Modern public transport can be fast and cheap.
  • Public transport can help to reduce pollution in cities.
  • Investment in buses and trains will ease traffic congestion.
  • Buses can be given special lanes to avoid traffic.

Negatives

  • Public transport is often slow and unreliable.
  • Metro systems and trains are often dirty and crowded.
  • People feel like “sardines in a can”.
  • Cars are much more comfortable.
  • Driving while tired or drunk is extremely dangerous.
  • Mobile phones can be a dangerous distraction for drivers.
  • They draw the driver’s attention away from the road.
  • The use of phones while driving has been banned in many countries.
  • Punishments are becoming stricter.
  • Television campaigns are used to remind people to drive safely.
  • Speed cameras have become more common.
  • Speed bumps are another form of traffic calming.
  • Many streets are designed with the aim of slowing traffic down.

Sample Answers Band 6.5–7.0

Part 1 — Transport

1. Are you interested in ways of travelling?

Yes, to some extent. I would not say I am an expert, but ways of travelling affects daily life, so I find it worth thinking about. For example, when I read news or talk with friends, this topic often comes up naturally.

2. Is ways of travelling important in your country?

Yes, definitely. In my country, ways of travelling matters because it influences families, young people, and the wider community. It may not be something people discuss every day, but it has a real impact on quality of life.

3. Has ways of travelling changed in recent years?

Yes, quite a lot. Technology, education, and modern lifestyles have changed the way people think about ways of travelling. Older people may have more traditional views, while younger people tend to be more open and practical.

4. Do young people care about ways of travelling?

I think many young people do care, especially when it affects their future directly. However, they may express interest through social media or online discussions rather than traditional conversations.

Part 2 — Cue card sample

A time i was stuck in a traffic jam

I would like to talk about a time I was stuck in a traffic jam, which is closely connected to ways of travelling.

I first became aware of it a few years ago, and since then it has stayed in my mind because it reflects both the positive and negative sides of modern life.

The positive side is that it shows the value of public transport and cleaner vehicles. This is important because people often need practical examples, not just abstract ideas, to understand a topic clearly.

At the same time, it also reminds me of the problem of traffic congestion and air pollution. That makes the topic more complex, because there is rarely a perfect solution.

Overall, I find it meaningful because it helps me think more deeply about society and my own choices. If I had to summarise my feeling, I would say it is both interesting and relevant to everyday life.

Part 3 — Discussion

1. Why is ways of travelling important?

It is important because it affects individuals and society at the same time. For example, public transport and cleaner vehicles can improve peoples quality of life, while traffic congestion and air pollution can create long-term problems. That is why this topic deserves more attention from both citizens and governments.

2. What are the advantages related to ways of travelling?

The main advantages are connected to public transport and cleaner vehicles. If this area is managed well, people can benefit through better opportunities, more convenience, and a higher standard of living. It can also help communities become more organised and forward-thinking.

3. What are the disadvantages or risks?

The biggest risk is traffic congestion and air pollution. If people ignore this issue, it can lead to social pressure, inequality, or environmental damage depending on the situation. In my view, the negative side should not be exaggerated, but it should be taken seriously.

4. What should governments do about ways of travelling?

Governments should create clear policies, invest in public education, and make sure rules are enforced fairly. For example, they can fund useful programmes and raise public awareness. However, government action alone is not enough; individuals also need to change their habits.

5. Do you think ways of travelling will change in the future?

Yes, I think it will continue to change, mainly because of technology, globalisation, and younger generations. People are becoming more aware of both benefits and risks. In the future, I expect more practical solutions rather than simple arguments for or against it.

6. Do older and younger people think differently about ways of travelling?

Yes, in many cases. Older people may value tradition, stability, and personal experience, while younger people are often influenced by online information and global trends. Neither side is completely right or wrong; the best approach is to combine experience with fresh ideas.
